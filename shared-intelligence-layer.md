# Shared Intelligence Layer

How to think about reusable intelligence across multiple AI products and workflows.

---

## What is a shared intelligence layer?

A shared intelligence layer is the reusable logic, context, and decision-making foundation that powers multiple product experiences.

Instead of each workflow independently solving the same intelligence problems, teams can build shared layers for:
- ranking
- retrieval
- personalization
- memory
- intent understanding
- policy decisions
- quality scoring

---

## Why it matters

Without a shared intelligence layer:
- workflows drift apart
- quality becomes inconsistent
- repeated work grows
- learning does not compound across products

With a shared intelligence layer:
- knowledge can compound
- capabilities can improve across products
- product teams move faster

---

## Examples

A shared intelligence layer may power:
- meeting prep
- outbound personalization
- support assistants
- internal search
- lead scoring
- content drafting

The UI is different.
The user goal is different.
But the intelligence layer can reuse:
- profile understanding
- context selection
- ranking logic
- feedback learning

---

## What belongs in the layer

Possible components:
- user/entity understanding
- context ranking
- quality scoring
- memory signals
- workflow intent classification
- retrieval preferences
- personalization rules

---

## What should not be centralized too early

- workflow-specific UX
- niche business rules
- product-specific interaction logic

---

## Final principle

A shared intelligence layer becomes valuable when it improves multiple workflows while preserving enough flexibility for product teams to optimize their user experience.
